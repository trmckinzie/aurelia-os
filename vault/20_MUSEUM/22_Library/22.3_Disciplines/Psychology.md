While evolutionary approaches focus on _ultimate_ causes (why traits exist), broader psychology often focuses on _proximate_ causes (how environment, culture, and personal history shape an individual right now). ### ðŸ”‘ Core Concepts (The Bricks)
- [[Evolutionary Psychology]]: The understanding that our mental faculties are adaptationsâ€”tools forged by natural selection to solve specific ancestral problems (e.g., mate selection, cheater detection).
- [[Cognitive Architecture]]: The structural design of the mind, often modeled as **Information Processing** (Input $\to$ Algorithm $\to$ Output). Includes the distinction between System 1 (Fast/Heuristic) and System 2 (Slow/Rational).
- [[Behaviorism & Conditioning]]: The study of how organisms learn through interaction with the environment via reinforcement (rewards) and punishment (costs). This is the "habit loop" layer.
- [[Neuroplasticity]]: The brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections throughout life; the mechanism by which "software" updates occur.
- [[Social Influence & Situationism]]: The study of how the immediate environment and group dynamics override individual personality. This covers conformity, obedience to authority (Milgram), and the "Fundamental Attribution Error"â€”our tendency to blame people's character rather than their context.
- [[The Unconscious & Defense Mechanisms]]: Rooted in Psychodynamics (Freud/Jung), this concept posits that the vast majority of mental activity occurs below conscious awareness. It explores how the mind protects itself from anxiety through mechanisms like repression, projection, and sublimation.
- [[Attachment Theory]]: The foundational model of emotional bonding (Bowlby/Ainsworth). It explains how early interactions with caregivers create "internal working models" that dictate how we trust, love, and relate to others throughout adulthood (Secure, Anxious, Avoidant styles).
- **[[The Big Five (OCEAN)]]:** The most empirically validated framework for Personality Psychology. It moves away from "types" (like MBTI) to measure individuals across five continuous dimensions: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism.
- **[[Humanism & Self-Actualization]]:** A counter-weight to the "biological machine" view. Pioneered by Maslow and Rogers, it focuses on the inherent human drive toward growth, meaning, and reaching full potential (Self-Actualization) once basic survival needs are met.
