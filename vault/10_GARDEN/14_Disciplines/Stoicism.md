### ðŸ§ Definition (The Scope)
> A Hellenistic school of philosophy founded by Zeno of Citium (c. 300 BC), designed to produce **Eudaimonia** (human flourishing) through the cultivation of **Virtue** (Excellence of Character). Unlike modern definitions of "stoicism" (suppressing emotion), the ancient philosophy is about _domestication_ of emotion through reason. It views philosophy as a practice consisting of three interconnected disciplines: **Physics** (understanding how the world works/nature), **Logic** (how to think clearly), and **Ethics** (how to live). It asserts that the only true good is a virtuous character, as it is the only thing that cannot be used for evil.
