### ðŸ’¡ The Core Argument (Thesis)
>The distinction between "facts" (science) and "values" (morality) is an illusion. Morality is strictly about the **well-being of conscious creatures**. Since "well-being" depends on the state of the human brain and the laws of physics/sociology, there are objective answers to moral questions, even if we do not possess them yet. Harris proposes a **Landscape of Morality**: a hypothetical space where "peaks" correspond to societies of maximal flourishing and "valleys" correspond to maximal suffering. Science can and should dictate which cultural practices lead to the peaks, effectively making moral relativism intellectually bankrupt.
